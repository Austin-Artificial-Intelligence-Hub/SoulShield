Executive Summary

SoulShield is a trauma-informed AI support companion designed to help survivors of human trafficking and domestic violence navigate their emotions between therapy sessions. Built with multiple layers of encryption and agentic AI, SoulShield addresses a critical barrier: survivors don't trust ChatGPT with their most sensitive questions.

The system was developed in collaboration with a survivor support organization in Austin, Texas, who confirmed this addresses a critical unmet need: survivors have no support during nights, weekends, and between therapy sessions when emotional distress is often most intense ‚Äî and existing LLMs feel too risky for their sensitive conversations.

The Gap in Mental Health Support

Survivors who escape trafficking face a long journey of healing. Professional therapy is essential ‚Äî but therapy sessions typically occur only once per week for one hour. What happens during the other 167 hours?

"If it happens after hours or on the weekend, we're obviously not here for that. The [survivors] do have a lot of emotional distress, especially in the first few weeks."

When survivors finally reach safety, something unexpected happens: their anxiety often increases. After years of survival mode, their body finally allows suppressed trauma to surface.

"When they come here and they are like, okay, now I'm safe, then that's when a lot of the aha moments and the flooding of real emotions come into play... their body's like, oh, finally I'm safe. And I actually get to process everything that's happened to me."

Why Survivors Don't Trust ChatGPT

AI chatbots like ChatGPT could theoretically help ‚Äî but survivors won't use them. Here's why:

üîí The Trust Barrier

  • Hypersensitivity to Privacy Risk: Survivors' trauma history makes them acutely aware of any potential exposure. Even if ChatGPT's data leak risk is statistically minimal, they psychologically cannot take that chance.
  • Extremely Sensitive Questions: Their conversations naturally involve the most private details ‚Äî "Was I drugged?", "Is what happened to me assault?", "I don't remember what happened that night." These are questions they would never type into a system owned by a tech company.
  • Fear of Conflicting Advice: What if the AI says something different from their therapist? This concern was raised directly by clinical staff: "My other concern would be that there would be conflict between what ChatGPT is telling them and what we're telling them."

Our Solution

SoulShield is built on two core principles: (1) Security survivors can actually trust, and (2) Supplement, never replace, professional therapy.

Breaking the Trust Barrier

We solved the privacy problem with multiple layers of encryption that make SoulShield fundamentally different from ChatGPT:

  • Client-Side Encryption: Messages are encrypted on the user's device before leaving their phone ‚Äî not on our servers
  • Zero-Knowledge Design: Even we cannot read their conversations
  • Own Infrastructure: Data stays on organization-controlled servers (via Microsoft for Nonprofits), not third-party systems
  • Auto-Delete: All data automatically purges after 30 days

Never Replacing the Therapist

Our agentic AI is designed with strict guardrails to avoid therapeutic conflict:

  • Never gives therapy advice ‚Äî only supports with grounding and emotion identification
  • Encourages users to bring insights to their next session
  • Explicitly positions itself as a supplement, not a replacement
  • Uses the same dialectical behavioral therapy (DBT) techniques staff already teach
"I always say, I think it's a good supplemental tool guide... ChatGPT has all the knowledge of all therapeutic modalities, so it could offer some sort of guidance and tools that could be helpful in a moment of mental health crisis."
‚Äî Clinical Staff, Survivor Support Organization

Three Modes of Support

  1. Grounding Mode ‚Äî Box breathing, 5-4-3-2-1 sensory exercises when overwhelmed. Uses the same techniques clinical staff teach: ice packs for vagus nerve, bilateral stimulation, sensory grounding.
  2. Emotion Exploration ‚Äî Helps name feelings without giving advice. Mirrors the "feelings wheel" approach used in therapy: "If somebody does not recognize what they're feeling, they don't know how to process those feelings."
  3. Session Preparation ‚Äî Organizes thoughts and identifies emotions for the next therapy appointment. Helps maximize the value of their one hour per week with their therapist.

Bystander-Safe Mode

Many survivors are still in dangerous situations. Some have abusers who monitor their devices. Others may be physically beside their trafficker while using the app. SoulShield handles both scenarios.

Scenario A: Device Monitoring

When users indicate someone might check their phone later ("He reads all my messages"), SoulShield automatically uses neutral wellness language:

User: "He checks my phone every night. I'm having a hard day."

SoulShield: "I hear you ‚Äî some days are just harder than others. Would you like to try a quick mindfulness moment together? Let's take a slow breath in... and out."

Notice: No words like "abuse," "escape," or "therapy." To anyone checking the phone, it looks like a generic wellness app.

Scenario B: Trafficker Physically Present

In the most dangerous situations, the trafficker may be sitting right beside them. The survivor needs support, information about their rights, and mental survival strategies ‚Äî but the trafficker is watching the screen. SoulShield communicates in a way the survivor understands but the trafficker won't suspect.

Would you like to try another breathing exercise, or would it help to think about something you're looking forward to?"

The agentic AI automatically detects privacy signals like "I can't talk freely," "Someone is nearby," or "Keep it simple" and adjusts its language accordingly ‚Äî providing mental support and subtle empowerment without endangering the survivor.

Key Prompt Optimization Insights

Through iterative testing with LangSmith, we discovered critical improvements:

  1. "Robotic Therapist" Problem: Early prompts produced scripted, clinical responses. We removed prescriptive rules and added instructions to mirror the user's natural language.
  2. Subtle Bystander Detection: Users don't say "someone is watching." They say "he checks my phone." We built a dataset of 39 real-world signals to train subtle detection.
  3. Avoiding Therapeutic Conflict: The support_coach prompt went through 8 iterations to ensure it never gives advice ‚Äî only supports, grounds, and prepares for therapy.
